More about Partitions​

When analyzing firm sales, it's often useful to group firms into a family of non-overlapping subsets called parts (similar to sectors). Both aggregate sales and their variance are defined in an analogous way for firms and sectors, with the only difference being the index used.

Here, the indices kkk represent firms and ppp represent parts. Partitions can be determined by various criteria, such as industry sectors, geographical regions, or even random allocation.

Footnote 1: Partitions can also be defined on the sets of buyer firms as well as seller firms, allowing for a network approach to cross-accounting of sales. Using indices ppp and rrr for parts, total sales from exchanges between parts and firms can be expressed as:

Types of Partitions​

Two types of partitions are particularly useful due to their formal properties:

  • Equal Weight Partitions: These are partitions where the value held by any PPP parts is the same, ideally Sˉp=Xˉ/P\bar S_p = \bar X/PSˉp​=Xˉ/P. While perfect precision may not be achievable, firms can often be separated into parts with weights close to Xˉ/P\bar X/PXˉ/P. Randomized assignments to parts can be referred to as random partitions.

  • Quantile Partitions: Also known as size-sorted equal weight partitions, these involve sorting firms by size before dividing them into QQQ groups. Each partition's total sales are near Sˉq=Xˉ/Q\bar S_q = \bar X/QSˉq​=Xˉ/Q. With a sufficient number of parts (e.g., Q=10,20Q = 10, 20Q=10,20), firms in most parts are close to a mean size (sˉq\bar s_qsˉq​).

For quantile partitions, we expect Sˉq=Xˉ/Q=sˉqnq\bar S_q = \bar X/Q = \bar s_q n_qSˉq​=Xˉ/Q=sˉq​nq​, where nqn_qnq​ is the number of firms in quantile qqq. The mean size sˉq\bar s_qsˉq​ increases monotonically across successive parts qqq, necessitating a decrease in quantile population nqn_qnq​ to maintain the quantile value near Sˉq=Xˉ/Q\bar S_q = \bar X/QSˉq​=Xˉ/Q. Quantile partitions thus offer defined agent and population sizes, making them highly useful for analyzing micro moments as functions of agent size and quantile qqq.
