Simple Structure of Sectoral Sales

Simple Structure of Sectoral Sales​

Assuming a structure for the time series of sectoral sales involves defining it as a sum of time series that partially compose it. For instance, fitting sectoral sales with a generic spts_{pt}spt​ results in:

Sp=β^pst+rptS_p = \hat \beta_p s_t + r_{pt}Sp​=β^​p​st​+rpt​

This leads to an expression for aggregate variance, which is the sum of elements of the cross covariance matrix:

var(X)=∑ijcov(Si,Sj)=∑ijcov(β^ist+rit,β^jst+rjt)var(X) = \sum_{ij} cov(S_i, S_j) = \sum_{ij} cov(\hat \beta_i s_t + r_{it}, \hat \beta_j s_t + r_{jt})var(X)=ij∑​cov(Si​,Sj​)=ij∑​cov(β^​i​st​+rit​,β^​j​st​+rjt​)

Here, β^p\hat \beta_pβ^​p​ are elements of β=(stTst)−1stTY\bm \beta = (s_t^T s_t)^{-1} s_t^T Yβ=(stT​st​)−1stT​Y, with Y∈RT×PY \in \mathbb{R}^{T \times P}Y∈RT×P containing the observed parts' time series and sts_tst​ as a column vector used for fitting. It can be z-standardized so that cov(st,st)=1cov(s_t, s_t) = 1cov(st​,st​)=1 (see Figure 4).

Note: A specific example is fitting (OLS) using a factor of linear time evolution st=ts_t = tst​=t, then: Spt=β^pt+rptS_{pt} = \hat \beta_{p} t + r_{pt}Spt​=β^​p​t+rpt​, where βp\beta_{p}βp​ is as described and rptr_{pt}rpt​ are the residuals of the fit.

The elements of the cross covariance matrix are expressed as:

cov(β^ist+rit,β^jst+rjt)=cov(β^ist,β^jst)+cov(β^ist,rjt)+cov(rit,β^jst)+cov(rit,rjt)\begin{split} cov(\hat \beta_i s_t + r_{it}, \hat \beta_j s_t + r_{jt}) = & cov(\hat \beta_i s_t, \hat \beta_j s_t) + cov(\hat \beta_i s_t, r_{jt}) \\ & + cov(r_{it}, \hat \beta_j s_t) + cov(r_{it}, r_{jt}) \end{split}cov(β^​i​st​+rit​,β^​j​st​+rjt​)=​cov(β^​i​st​,β^​j​st​)+cov(β^​i​st​,rjt​)+cov(rit​,β^​j​st​)+cov(rit​,rjt​)​

Each of the P×PP \times PP×P elements is composed of four components. Generally, a structure of sectoral sales made from NNN terms results in N×NN \times NN×N terms forming each element of the cross covariance matrix.

The β^\hat \betaβ^​ coefficients are arranged into a column vector β^\bm{\hat \beta}β^​ of length PPP, and the standard deviation of residuals σ\sigmaσ in the column vector σ^\bm{\hat \sigma}σ^ of length PPP. The matrix product β^β^T\bm{\hat \beta} \bm{\hat \beta}^Tβ^​β^​T creates a P×PP \times PP×P matrix (an outer product), and the cross covariance matrix among sectors is expressed as:

C=β^β^T⋅C(st,st)+β^σT⋅C(st,ϵt)+σβ^T⋅C(ϵt,st)+σσT⋅C(ϵt,ϵt)\begin{split} C = & \bm{\hat \beta} \bm{\hat \beta}^T \cdot C(s_t, s_t) + \bm{\hat \beta} \bm{\sigma}^T \cdot C(s_t, \epsilon_{t}) \\ & + \bm{\sigma} \bm{\hat \beta}^T \cdot C(\epsilon_{t}, s_t) + \bm{\sigma} \bm{\sigma}^T \cdot C(\epsilon_{t}, \epsilon_{t}) \end{split}C=​β^​β^​T⋅C(st​,st​)+β^​σT⋅C(st​,ϵt​)+σβ^​T⋅C(ϵt​,st​)+σσT⋅C(ϵt​,ϵt​)​

where C(a,b)C({a}, {b})C(a,b) denotes the P×PP \times PP×P cross covariance matrix computed from vectors aaa and bbb. They are multiplied with the outer product matrices element-wise.

In this context, aggregate variance comprises a comovement contribution and an idiosyncrasies contribution, apart from cross terms that often do not significantly contribute.

Note: If the time series of the parts are independent, the expectation is E[var[X]]=∑kσk2E[var[X]] = \sum_k \sigma^2_kE[var[X]]=∑k​σk2​. The cross covariance terms are, on average, zero, although it is likely that none of them is truly null. They will add up to the uncertainty with which we can estimate σ2\sigma^2σ2 as ∑kσk2\sum_k \sigma^2_k∑k​σk2​. We maintain cross covariance terms even when their total expectation is zero.
