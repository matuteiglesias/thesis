Acknowledging Entry and Exit Events

Acknowledging Entry and Exit Events​

In this section, we explore the dynamics of entry and exit events in firms and their impact on sectoral sales time series. This is crucial for extending the formal framework to account for the active or inactive status of firms over time.

Consider the decomposition of sectoral sales time series SptS_{pt}Spt​:

Spt=Sˉp+ΔSpt=Sp0+Bpt+Mpt+EptS_{pt} = \bar S_p + \Delta S_{pt} = S^0_p + B_{pt} + M_{pt} + E_{pt}Spt​=Sˉp​+ΔSpt​=Sp0​+Bpt​+Mpt​+Ept​
  • Sˉp\bar S_pSˉp​: The observed mean of the time series.
  • ΔSpt\Delta S_{pt}ΔSpt​: Deviations from the mean level.
  • BptB_{pt}Bpt​: Accounts for nominal fluctuations, especially due to entry and exit events.
  • MptM_{pt}Mpt​: Captures contributions related to comovements.
  • EptE_{pt}Ept​: Captures contributions related to sectoral idiosyncrasies.

The relationship implies Spt−Sp0=Bpt+Mpt+EptS_{pt} - S^0_p = B_{pt} + M_{pt} + E_{pt}Spt​−Sp0​=Bpt​+Mpt​+Ept​, where Sp0S^0_pSp0​ is observed when all fluctuations are removed. Note that Sˉp\bar S_pSˉp​ includes the net mean of the B,M,EB, M, EB,M,E terms.

Given that Xt=∑pSpX_t = \sum_p S_pXt​=∑p​Sp​, the variance is:

σ2(Xt)≡∑i,j∈PCov(Si,Sj)\sigma^2(X_t) \equiv \sum\limits_{i, j \in P} Cov(S_i, S_j)σ2(Xt​)≡i,j∈P∑​Cov(Si​,Sj​)

The covariance decomposition is:

cov(Bit+Mit+Eit,Bjt+Mjt+Ejt)=cov(Bit,Bjt)+cov(Bit,Mjt)+cov(Bit,Ejt)+cov(Mit,Bjt)+cov(Mit,Mjt)+cov(Mit,Ejt)+cov(Eit,Bjt)+cov(Eit,Mjt)+cov(Eit,Ejt)\begin{split} cov(B_{it} + M_{it} + E_{it}, B_{jt} + M_{jt} + E_{jt}) = & cov(B_{it}, B_{jt}) + cov(B_{it}, M_{jt}) + cov(B_{it}, E_{jt}) \\ + & cov(M_{it}, B_{jt}) + cov(M_{it}, M_{jt}) + cov(M_{it}, E_{jt}) \\ + & cov(E_{it}, B_{jt}) + cov(E_{it}, M_{jt}) + cov(E_{it}, E_{jt}) \end{split}cov(Bit​+Mit​+Eit​,Bjt​+Mjt​+Ejt​)=++​cov(Bit​,Bjt​)+cov(Bit​,Mjt​)+cov(Bit​,Ejt​)cov(Mit​,Bjt​)+cov(Mit​,Mjt​)+cov(Mit​,Ejt​)cov(Eit​,Bjt​)+cov(Eit​,Mjt​)+cov(Eit​,Ejt​)​

Log fluctuations can be similarly decomposed:

log⁡(Spt)=log⁡(Sˉp)+Fpt=log⁡(Sp0)+bpt+mpt+σpϵpt\log(S_{pt}) = \log(\bar S_p) + F_{pt} = \log(S^0_p) + b_{pt} + m_{pt} + \sigma_p \epsilon_{pt}log(Spt​)=log(Sˉp​)+Fpt​=log(Sp0​)+bpt​+mpt​+σp​ϵpt​
  • ϵpt\epsilon_{pt}ϵpt​: A random variable with mean zero and std = 1.

This implies:

log⁡(Spt/Sp0)=bpt+mpt+σpϵpt\log(S_{pt}/S^0_p) = b_{pt} + m_{pt} + \sigma_p \epsilon_{pt}log(Spt​/Sp0​)=bpt​+mpt​+σp​ϵpt​

The relationship to nominal accounts is:

Spt=Sp0+Bpt+Mpt+Ept=Sp010bpt+mpt+σpϵptS_{pt} = S^0_p + B_{pt} + M_{pt} + E_{pt} = S^0_p 10^{b_{pt} + m_{pt} + \sigma_{p} \epsilon_{pt}}Spt​=Sp0​+Bpt​+Mpt​+Ept​=Sp0​10bpt​+mpt​+σp​ϵpt​

Consider a thought experiment where fluctuations are 'turned on' from zero to actual magnitudes. Initially, log⁡(Spt/Sp0)=bpt\log(S_{pt}/S^0_p) = b_{pt}log(Spt​/Sp0​)=bpt​. As shocks are activated, we observe differences as:

log⁡(Spt′)−log⁡(Spt)=δpt=mpt+σpϵpt\log(S'_{pt}) - \log(S_{pt}) = \delta_{pt} = m_{pt} + \sigma_p \epsilon_{pt}log(Spt′​)−log(Spt​)=δpt​=mpt​+σp​ϵpt​

The width of this time series is σ=σδ2+σb2\sigma = \sqrt{\sigma_{\delta}^2 + \sigma_b^2}σ=σδ2​+σb2​​. Depending on the magnitude of micro shocks and firm dynamics, different sources may explain observed aggregate volatility.

For mild sectoral fluctuations, the covariance matrix elements are:

cov(log⁡(Sit),log⁡(Sjt))=cov(bit+mit+σiϵit,bjt+mjt+σjϵjt)=cov(bit,bjt)+cov(bit,mjt)+cov(bit,σjϵjt)+cov(mit,bjt)+cov(mit,mjt)+cov(mit,σjϵjt)+cov(σiϵit,bjt)+cov(σiϵit,mjt)+cov(σiϵit,σjϵjt)\begin{split} cov(\log(S_{it}), \log(S_{jt})) = & cov(b_{it} + m_{it} + \sigma_i \epsilon_{it}, b_{jt} + m_{jt} + \sigma_j \epsilon_{jt}) \\ = & cov(b_{it}, b_{jt}) + cov(b_{it}, m_{jt}) + cov(b_{it}, \sigma_j \epsilon_{jt}) \\ + & cov(m_{it}, b_{jt}) + cov(m_{it}, m_{jt}) + cov(m_{it}, \sigma_j \epsilon_{jt}) \\ + & cov(\sigma_i \epsilon_{it}, b_{jt}) + cov(\sigma_i \epsilon_{it}, m_{jt}) + cov(\sigma_i \epsilon_{it}, \sigma_j \epsilon_{jt}) \end{split}cov(log(Sit​),log(Sjt​))==++​cov(bit​+mit​+σi​ϵit​,bjt​+mjt​+σj​ϵjt​)cov(bit​,bjt​)+cov(bit​,mjt​)+cov(bit​,σj​ϵjt​)cov(mit​,bjt​)+cov(mit​,mjt​)+cov(mit​,σj​ϵjt​)cov(σi​ϵit​,bjt​)+cov(σi​ϵit​,mjt​)+cov(σi​ϵit​,σj​ϵjt​)​

Each element involves nine terms, combinations of b,m,σϵb, m, \sigma\epsilonb,m,σϵ. For PPP parts, the total variance sums 9P29P^29P2 contributions, with different combinations and cross terms contributing to the aggregate variance.
