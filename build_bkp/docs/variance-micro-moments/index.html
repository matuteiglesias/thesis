Nonlinearities and the Law of Large Numbers​

The σ2\sigma^2σ2 contribution shows cancellation of opposite shocks and convergence of the mean, as when averaging a time series showing additive deviations from a level. Both log-normal and log-Laplace shocks contain this σ^2\hat \sigma^2σ^2 dependence. However, these multiplicative micro shocks have additional higher-order terms o(σ^4)o(\hat \sigma^4)o(σ^4) that grow as micro fluctuations are turned on. These nonlinearities make multiplicative shocks different from additive Gaussian shocks and are particularly stronger if micro shocks are fat-tailed (log-Laplace).
